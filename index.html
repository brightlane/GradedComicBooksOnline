Appraisal Methodology

Each of the 350 CGC-graded books was individually appraised using auction data, CGC census scarcity, grade differentials and collector demand patterns as of 2024. Values represent fair-market estimates — not guaranteed sale prices.

Auction Comparables

Where recent sales exist at similar or nearby grades, those prices anchor the appraisal with appropriate grade adjustments.

Census Scarcity

"Only book on CGC Census" books receive a significant premium — typically 2-4x comparable non-census books.

Grade Adjustment

Each CGC grade point typically represents 15-30% of value. Books graded higher than a reference sale are valued at a premium, and vice versa.

Restoration Discount

Restored books (Slight C-1, C-2 etc.) are valued at 40-70% below equivalent unrestored copies.

Key Issue Premium

First appearances, origins and milestone issues carry 20-50% premiums above non-key books of equivalent grade.

Error Labels

CGC mislabeled books (wrong title) are unique collector items — their error status can add or reduce value depending on demand.
